Geospatial Analysis of Solar Energy as Electricity Source in the United States

Medy Xu, Eldi Rahmadan, Muhammad Aliy, Lianxin Wang, Guan He, Wangqi Li

Overview:

  • Global warming issues and the rise of renewable energy during the past decade.
  • 18% of all electricity in the United States was produced by renewable sources such as solar power
  • Solar power is more affordable, accessible, and prevalent in the United States than ever before.
  • The installations of solar facilities have grown fast
  • The cost of solar panels and solar electric systems decreasing sharply

Objectives:

Making a set of maps that help policy makers find the patterns and effective measures to boost solar energy development nationwide by answering:

  • What is the current status of solar energy within that state?
  • What are some binding factors on developing solar energy within different states?
  • What is the reason that some states, albeit having great policy support or sunlight radiation, do not produce solar power up to an expected amount?

Data Sources:

  • Data.Gov U.S. State Boundaries
  • National Renewable Energy Laboratory Solar Summaries
